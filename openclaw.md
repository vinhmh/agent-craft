# OpenClaw — quick notes

## What it is

**OpenClaw** is an AI agent that learns how real software looks and behaves. It has been trained on many images of operating systems and applications across lots of devices, so it understands UIs the way a person does when they look at a screen.

## What it can do

It can **use applications on your OS like a normal user**: clicking, typing, navigating menus, and following on-screen workflows instead of only talking in text.

## Why “local” matters

OpenClaw can run in **your own environment** — for example on **your computer** or on **a server you control**. That is a practical difference from many cloud-only assistants: your workflow and data stay **on your machine or your infra**, instead of being sent off to large third-party services by default. (Exact behavior still depends on how you configure it and which features you turn on.)

## Community and skills

OpenClaw has a **large community**. People can **author skills** (reusable know-how for specific apps or tasks), **publish them to the cloud**, and others can **download and use** those skills with little friction — so the ecosystem grows beyond what any one person builds alone.
